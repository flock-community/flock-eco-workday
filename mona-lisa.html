The Mona Lisa stands as perhaps the most celebrated portrait in the history of art. Leonardo's masterful use of sfumato—the subtle gradation of tone and color—creates an ethereal quality that has captivated viewers for centuries. Her enigmatic smile, the mysterious landscape behind her, and the revolutionary three-quarter pose combine to create an image of haunting beauty and psychological depth. The portrait transcends its Renaissance origins to become a universal symbol of artistic achievement and human expression.
